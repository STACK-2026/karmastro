## 1. What Mercury retrograde actually is

You have heard of it. On social media, in conversations between friends, in an Instagram story, someone always ends up saying: "It's normal, Mercury is retrograde." As if that single sentence were enough to explain a lost email, an argument, a canceled train, or an ex who reappears.

Mercury retrograde is probably the most publicized, most feared, and most misunderstood astrological transit in popular culture. People blame it for every misfortune, cancel projects, delay decisions, accuse this poor little planet of everything that goes wrong in their lives. The truth is more subtle, and more useful.

In this article, we are going to put things back in perspective. Not to minimize the phenomenon, because it is very real and has a measurable impact on certain areas of life. But to give you a clear, dust-off reading, and to let you transform these three annual periods into allies rather than nightmares to be endured holding your breath.

## 2. The celestial mechanics: why a planet appears to move backward

First thing to know, and it matters: Mercury never actually moves backward. No planet ever reverses its course in the sky. What we observe is an optical illusion, exactly like when you pass a car on the highway and it looks like it is moving backward, even though it is still going forward.

Mercury, closer to the Sun than Earth, orbits faster than we do. Periodically, it catches up with us and overtakes us. During this overtaking, seen from Earth, it appears to slow down, stop, then move backward through the zodiac, before stopping again and resuming its forward course. This apparent motion is what we call retrograde.

Ancient astronomers knew this phenomenon already. They had noticed that all planets, except the Sun and the Moon, had moments when they seemed to hesitate and reverse. They turned these into powerful symbolic markers. And that is where the entire astrological tradition around retrogrades comes from: not from a naive belief, but from a symbolic reading of a real motion observed for thousands of years.

For Mercury specifically, this retrograde lasts about three weeks, and it happens three times a year. Before and after this phase of apparent reversed motion, there are two more discreet but important periods called the shadow phases, which we will come back to.

## 3. Three times a year, always in the same element

Here is something many people do not know: Mercury does not retrograde just anywhere. Each year, its three retrogrades tend to happen in signs of the same element. One year in air signs, another in water signs, another in fire signs, another in earth signs, and so on.

This gives a global coloring to the year. When Mercury retrogrades in fire signs, the questions revisited are about momentum, identity, spontaneity. In earth signs, it is about the concrete, money, the body, work. In air signs, communication, ideas, relationships. In water signs, emotions, memory, family and intimate ties.

This is not trivial. If you feel particularly shaken by one retrograde and not the next, it may simply be because your natal chart resonates strongly with the element where it unfolds. A Sun or rising sign in Aquarius will be more shaken by a retrograde in air signs, for example. To know, [calculate your rising sign](/en/tools/rising-sign) and cross-reference with the affected signs.

In 2026, for instance, Mercury retrogrades concentrate mostly in water signs and a bit of air, which explains why this year is particularly intense emotionally for many people, with memory resurgences, returns from the past, affective cycles closing.

## 4. Mercury, messenger of the gods and ruler of your mind

Before understanding what retrograde triggers, you need to know what Mercury represents in astrological language. Mercury is the smallest planet in the solar system, the closest to the Sun, the fastest. In Roman mythology, it is the messenger of the gods, the equivalent of the Greek Hermes: a cunning, mobile god, gifted with language, commerce, negotiation, travel, but also lying, trickery, and the passage between worlds.

In astrology, Mercury rules everything related to the circulation of information. First, your way of thinking, learning, reasoning. How you structure your ideas, how you process data, your style of memory, your logic, your curiosity.

Mercury also rules communication in the broadest sense: speech, writing, messages, contracts, negotiations, announcements, teaching. And then everything that physically circulates: daily commutes, short trips, encounters, mail, packages, and more broadly all flows of transport and exchange.

Finally, Mercury is the natural ruler of Gemini and Virgo. In Gemini, it expresses itself in an agile, curious, butterfly-like, multi-directional way. In Virgo, it becomes meticulous, precise, analytical, attentive to detail and to the practical functioning of things. Its position in your natal chart says a great deal about your relationship to knowledge, speech, and exchange.

When this planet enters apparent reverse motion, all these areas are invited to pause, withdraw, review, readjust. Hence the sometimes uncomfortable feeling that everything suddenly jams up.

## 5. The real areas affected by retrograde

There is a big difference between what pop culture attributes to Mercury retrograde and what serious astrology says. Here are, without dramatization, the areas where the effect is statistically most visible.

First, interpersonal communication. Misunderstandings become more frequent. A misread email, a tone misinterpreted in a message, a conversation that derails because each person hears something different from what the other meant. It is not magic. It is more as if attention slipped on a detail that changed the entire meaning of a sentence.

Then, contracts and written commitments. Not that you should refuse to sign anything for three weeks, but it is a period where you should reread twice, ask a third party to verify, and where administrative errors are more frequent: a swapped digit, a forgotten date, a misread clause.

Then come the trips. Late trains, delayed flights, traffic jams, GPS that crashes, lost keys. Again, this does not happen every day, and not everyone is affected the same way, but statistically the logistical glitches multiply.

Finally, and this is perhaps the most interesting, returns from the past. Old colleagues who reach out to you, exes who reappear, old files resurfacing, buried projects you have to relaunch. Mercury retrograde is a great specialist in "look, this is back." And that function is precious, because it lets you close unfinished things.

## 6. The famous myths (and what is true in them)

Let us sort it out. Here are the most widespread beliefs, and what to actually think of them.

"You should not sign anything during Mercury retrograde." False in absolute terms. Life does not stop for three weeks, three times a year. What is true is that you should reread carefully and not sign in a hurry something you have not had time to analyze.

"You should not buy electronics." Nuanced. The idea comes from the fact that Mercury also rules machines and the circulation of information, hence anything tech. It is not that your computer will die because it was bought on a bad day, but it is a period when you can rush onto a model you will regret. Take your time.

"You should not travel." False. Otherwise, millions of people would be stuck at home three times a year. What is true is that trips during this phase can include more unexpected events, so anticipate: check your tickets, leave margin, keep backup plans handy.

"You always see an ex again." Nuanced. Many people do report romantic returns during these periods, but it is not systematic, and it is certainly not a sign from destiny. It is more an invitation to close something, not necessarily to start over.

"Everything goes wrong for three weeks." False. Most people feel nothing spectacular. If you do not have Mercury heavily activated in your chart, these periods can pass almost unnoticed.

What is broadly true, on the other hand, is that these phases are less suited to launching new things and more suited to finalizing, reworking, picking up, and revisiting questions you thought were settled.

## 7. The phases: shadow, station, retrograde, direct station, post-shadow

A Mercury retrograde is not a binary event that switches on and off. It is a cycle in five phases, each with its own color.

The pre-retrograde shadow phase begins about two weeks before the retrograde station. Mercury enters the zone of the zodiac it will travel three times during the cycle. This is often when the first information appears, the kind that will feed what follows. You receive an email, you meet someone, you start thinking about a topic: all of this will be replayed during the retrograde.

The retrograde station is the moment when Mercury seems to stop in the sky before going backward. It is the peak intensity of the cycle. Everything feels suspended, you may feel a mental fog, fatigue, difficulty deciding. This moment lasts two to three days.

The retrograde phase itself lasts about three weeks. Mercury symbolically moves backward through the signs. This is the most well-known and most publicized period. It is where returns, revisions, readjustments, and useful or annoying reversals happen, depending on the case.

The direct station, at the end, is the moment when Mercury seems to stop again before resuming its forward march. Two or three more days of intensity, sometimes of confusion, sometimes on the contrary of relief when something blocked finally unblocks.

Finally, the post-retrograde shadow phase lasts another two weeks after the end of the retrograde. Mercury passes a third time over the same zone of the zodiac, but in direct motion this time. This is when things clarify for good, decisions get made, and new information integrates.

In total, the full Mercury retrograde cycle therefore stretches over about two months, not just three small weeks. This matters for understanding why some effects persist before and after the official phase.

## 8. Mercury retrograde in each sign: what gets revisited

The sign in which Mercury retrogrades deeply colors what surfaces. Here is a quick overview.

**In Aries**, what gets replayed is momentum, spontaneity, taking initiative. You may wonder if you charged too fast somewhere, or hesitated too long. Unspoken anger resurfaces, projects launched in a rush ask to be readjusted.

**In Taurus**, material questions, the relationship to the body, money, security. You may review your budget, realize that a subscription costs more than you thought, renegotiate a rent, revisit your concrete values.

**In Gemini**, Mercury is at home. The retrograde is particularly intense for communication. Conversations to revisit, misunderstandings to clear, siblings showing up, lost friends reappearing.

**In Cancer**, emotions and family come to the front. Childhood memories, old family files, the relationship to the mother, sense of belonging. Very introspective period.

**In Leo**, self-image, creativity, heart projects. You may wonder if you are really playing your part, if you are showing up as you are, if your passion projects are aligned.

**In Virgo**, Mercury is also at home. A period suited for sorting, revising routines, putting files in order. Very productive in deep work, but sometimes mentally exhausting.

**In Libra**, relationships are revisited. Couples, partnerships, contracts. Conversations to have, balances to revisit, imbalances to name.

**In Scorpio**, what surfaces is powerful. Secrets, transformations, intimacy, shared money, sexuality. Intense period, sometimes draining, but deeply liberating if you play along.

**In Sagittarius**, beliefs, worldviews, travels, studies. You may question convictions, rediscover a faith, abandon another, revise a study project.

**In Capricorn**, the professional path, place in society, relationship to authority. Career to readjust, status to clarify, ambitions to reformulate.

**In Aquarius**, friendships, groups, collective projects, vision of the future. Networks to reconfigure, cause to rethink, place in a community to redefine.

**In Pisces**, Mercury is in fall, that is, in a delicate position. The retrograde is particularly blurry there. Intense dreams, confused intuitions, need for retreat, heightened sensitivity. Do not sign anything without rereading three times.

## 9. Transits on your natal chart: who is concerned

Everyone lives through Mercury retrograde, but not everyone feels it the same way. What makes the difference is your personal natal chart.

You will be particularly affected if Mercury retrograde lands on an important planet of your chart: your Sun, your Moon, your ascendant, your natal Mercury itself, or a sensitive point like your Midheaven or Descendant. In that case, the three weeks can be marked, sometimes annoying, sometimes deeply useful.

You will also be affected if the retrograde occurs in an active astrological house of your chart, that is, an area of life that already contains important planets. A retrograde in your seventh house if you have your Sun there, for example, will clearly shake your couple or your partnerships.

If the retrograde happens in an empty and quiet zone of your chart, and it does not touch any personal planet, you may very well feel nothing. It is that simple. This is why some friends will tell you "this retrograde was hell" while you have not noticed anything: your charts are different.

## 10. Mercury retrograde in your natal chart (born with it)

There is another dimension that is often overlooked: you may have been born with Mercury retrograde in your chart. About 18 to 20 percent of the world's population has a natal Mercury retrograde. It is not a curse, it is not even a flaw, it is just a particularity of mental functioning.

Natal Mercury retrograde people often have a non-linear way of thinking. They take a side road to arrive at an idea, they integrate information in spirals rather than in a straight line. They sometimes need more time to formulate something out loud, because their thinking circles around several times before coming out.

Many great artists, writers, philosophers, and researchers were born with Mercury retrograde. It often gives a deep interiority, an ability to think things differently, to dig, to reformulate, to find angles others do not see.

If you are a natal Mercury retrograde, the collective retrograde periods are not a nightmare for you. On the contrary, that is often when you feel most in your element, most creative, most at ease in your own rhythm. While others complain, you breathe.

## 11. Communication, contracts, travel: practical guide

Here are the good reflexes to adopt during the three weeks of retrograde, without falling into paranoia.

For **communication**, slow down before sending an important message. Reread it, ask yourself if the tone could be misinterpreted, if all the information is there, if you forgot an attachment. Favor face-to-face conversations when possible, rather than written exchanges that lend themselves to misunderstanding. And if you have a delicate discussion to have, do not wait three weeks to do it, but prepare it better.

For **contracts**, read every line. Ask a third party to reread if the stakes are high. Check the dates, the numbers, the names, the cancellation conditions. Do not sign in a rush a document handed to you five minutes before a meeting. If you can postpone the signature by a few days, do it. If you cannot, just be vigilant.

For **trips**, anticipate. Check your tickets the day before, leave margin, save your itineraries offline, keep your phone charged. Do not plan an ultra-tight trip between two meetings. And if something goes wrong (canceled train, delayed flight), take it philosophically: it is not the end of the world, it is just Mercury reminding you that you are not the absolute master of your schedule.

For **important purchases** related to communication or tech (phone, computer, car, appliances), if you can wait until the end of the retrograde, wait. If you cannot, make your choice with a little more distance than usual, read reviews, compare, do not rush.

## 12. Technology, machines, and unexpected events

Mercury rules everything that transmits information, and that includes our modern devices. During retrograde phases, many people report breakdowns, bugs, lost data, finicky wifi, updates that crash.

Should you make a drama of it? No. Should you take precautions? Yes. Here are some useful reflexes to adopt before and during the retrograde.

Back up your important data on an external drive or a cloud. Do not run a critical update on a system you use to work without first having tested that it is stable. If a device starts showing signs of weakness, leave it alone and do not push it with new installations. Avoid changing providers or migrating an important account if it is not vital.

None of this is magical. It is simply digital hygiene, but it takes on a particular meaning during these phases when collective attention is more distracted and small cascading errors happen more easily.

## 13. The hidden opportunities of Mercury retrograde

What if we flipped the script? Mercury retrograde is not a curse, it is an invitation. An invitation to slow down, to finish what is dragging, to pick up what we set aside. In a society that values only forward motion, advancement, progress, these phases force us to practice the art of going back, and that is precious.

Here is what you can do **actively** to take advantage of a Mercury retrograde rather than endure it.

Pick up an unfinished project. You know, the one you started six months ago and never finished. This is exactly the moment. Mercury retrograde loves returns, give it material to feed on.

Revisit a decision you did not have time to analyze. If you chose something in a hurry a few weeks ago, take these three weeks to reevaluate it consciously.

Reach back out to someone you have lost touch with. An old friend, a former colleague, a cousin you no longer see. It is often during these moments that reconnections become fluid.

Sort your archives. Digital or physical. Emails, photos, papers, old documents. Mercury retrograde loves sorting.

Rewrite something. A text, a project, a CV, a letter. The retrograde phase is ideal for revising, redoing, polishing, while the direct phase is ideal for publishing and sending.

Meditate on a recurring question. Something that comes back often in your thoughts and you never really looked at directly. This is the right moment to sit with it.

And above all, observe. Watch what comes back, listen to what those returns have to say to you, ask yourself why now. Mercury retrograde is a great revealer of unfinished loops. It is by paying attention that you close them.

Camille, 31, freelance journalist in Lyon. She comes to see us a little panicked three days before the start of a Mercury retrograde in Gemini. She is in the middle of writing a major investigation, she is waiting for a book contract, and she has to fly to Berlin the following week for a report.

Her natal chart shows Mercury in Gemini conjunct her Sun, and the retrograde will pass exactly over it. Suffice to say she is fully concerned. But rather than canceling everything as some of her friends suggested, we prepared a plan together.

For her investigation, we decided that she would use these three weeks to revisit all her sources, verify each quote, cross-check the information she had gathered a bit hastily. Rather than writing new chapters, she rewrote the existing ones. By the end of the retrograde, her text was twice as solid.

For her book contract, she asked her agent to read each clause aloud with her. They found two points that were problematic, which they had modified before signing. Without that vigilance, Camille would have signed without seeing.

For her trip to Berlin, she anticipated. Tickets printed in addition to the phone, external battery, comfortable margin between airport and meeting, local contact warned of her arrival. On the day, her flight was indeed thirty minutes late, but she arrived on time because she had built in margin.

And then something unexpected happened. During the retrograde, a former editor-in-chief with whom she had worked five years earlier reached out to her. He was launching a new media outlet and was looking for someone for a regular column. Camille accepted. She wrote recently that she wonders how she could have dreaded these three weeks, which turned out to be among the most productive and most beautiful of her year.

Astrology decides nothing for you. It gives you a frame, a calendar, a reading of the energies at play. It is up to you to do something with it. Camille turned it into a strength, and you can do the same.

## 15. Ritual and mental hygiene during retrograde

To finish this crossing, here are some simple practices that can really change your experience of a Mercury retrograde. No grand ceremonies, just mental and energetic hygiene.

**The journal of returns.** During the three weeks of retrograde, take five minutes in the evening to write what came back during your day. A thought you thought was forgotten. A face. A project. A feeling. Just note, without judging, without commenting. At the end of the retrograde, reread these pages and look at the patterns that emerge. It is often striking.

**The communication pause.** Once a day, for half an hour, cut everything. Phone on airplane mode, computer closed, no notifications. Just you, your breath, and your inner silence. It is in these pauses that real ideas surface.

**The unsent letter.** If someone from the past occupies your mind during the retrograde, write them a letter. Everything you have to say. Everything you never dared to say. And do not send it. Keep it, or burn it. The writing alone is enough to release what needs to be released, without you having to reopen an old door that does not need to be opened.

**The symbolic tidying.** Sort a drawer, a folder, a corner of your bedroom. Not everything, just one. Mercury retrograde loves sorting, and the space you free up in your environment frees up an equivalent space in your head.

**The flow meditation.** If you are used to meditating, orient your practice toward observing your thoughts during these three weeks. Do not try to stop them, watch them flow, see where they come from and where they go. Mercury loves to be observed.

**Water, lots of water.** Mercury and water are not symbolically linked in the classical sense, but water helps fluidify the circulation of information in the body and the mind. Drink more than usual during these periods, and you will feel the difference.

With these few practices, and with a clear reading of what is at play, Mercury retrograde becomes what it has always been in living astrological tradition: not an enemy, but a great teacher in the pedagogy of return.

## 16. FAQ Mercury retrograde

**How long does Mercury retrograde last?**
The strict retrograde phase lasts about three weeks. But the full cycle, including the shadow phases before and after, stretches over about two months.

**How many times a year does Mercury retrograde?**
Three times a year on average, sometimes four. It is the most frequent retrograde transit in the solar system.

**Does everyone feel Mercury retrograde?**
No. The people who feel it most are those whose natal chart is touched by the exact position of the retrograde Mercury, and more broadly people whose Mercury is very active in the chart (Gemini, Virgo, Mercury conjunct Sun, Gemini or Virgo rising).

**Can you sign a contract during Mercury retrograde?**
Yes, provided you read carefully and do not sign in a rush. It is not a ban, it is an invitation to vigilance.

**Should I cancel a trip planned during this period?**
No. Just anticipate, leave margin, keep backup plans. Millions of people travel during Mercury retrograde without major incident.

**Why does my ex always reach out during these periods?**
Mercury retrograde reactivates unfinished loops. If your ex returns, it is not a sign from destiny. It is more an invitation to cleanly close what was not closed. Reply, or do not reply, but do not take that return for anything other than a cycle closing.

**Does Mercury retrograde bring bad luck?**
No. No planetary position brings bad luck in itself. Mercury retrograde is a phase of pause, return, revision. What you do with it is up to you.

**Was I born with Mercury retrograde?**
You can check by looking at your natal chart. About 18 to 20 percent of people are born with a natal Mercury retrograde. If it is your case, these periods are not a nightmare for you. On the contrary, they are often your most creative moments.

**What to do if a fight breaks out during Mercury retrograde?**
Breathe before responding. Ask yourself if you understood what the other was really trying to say. Rephrase. Ask questions instead of asserting. And if possible, wait 24 hours before reacting to the most hurtful things.

**Does Mercury retrograde affect my health?**
Indirectly, yes. The stress linked to misunderstandings, mental fatigue, and the sense that everything is jamming can weigh on the nervous system. Take care of your sleep, hydrate, give yourself breaks. The body is in solidarity with the mind.
