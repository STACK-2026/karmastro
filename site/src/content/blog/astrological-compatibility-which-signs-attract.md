## What astrological compatibility really means

When people talk about **astrological compatibility**, most think of the classic cliche "Leo + Sagittarius = perfect" or "Scorpio + Taurus = passion." These associations based only on sun signs are a caricature. Real astrological compatibility is called **[synastry](/en/tools/synastry)**, and it consists in fully comparing two birth charts to understand the dynamic of a couple.

The term comes from ancient Greek *syn-astron*, literally "stars together." This discipline has been practiced since at least the second century AD, when Claudius Ptolemy already mentioned it in his *Tetrabiblos*. Medieval Arab astrologers refined the method, and figures like **Kepler** even calculated synastries for patrons of the imperial court.

One important point before going further: **synastry does not predict the success or failure of a relationship**. It maps zones of ease and zones of friction, it identifies the natural strengths of the couple and the challenges that will require conscious work. Two people with a "tense" synastry can live a magnificent story if they agree to grow together. Two people with a "harmonious" synastry can separate if neither of them nourishes the relationship. Astrology lights the ground, but you are the one walking.

## The 4 elements and their natural attractions

The zodiac is divided into **four elements** corresponding to four fundamental energies. Each element groups three signs, and these signs share strong natural affinities.

| Element | Signs | Dominant energy | Relational needs |
|---------|-------|-----------------|------------------|
| Fire | Aries, Leo, Sagittarius | Action, enthusiasm, inspiration | Freedom, adventure, stimulation |
| Earth | Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn | Concretization, sensuality, stability | Security, reliability, long term |
| Air | Gemini, Libra, Aquarius | Thought, communication, sociability | Dialogue, lightness, mental space |
| Water | Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces | Emotion, intuition, depth | Intimacy, fusion, tacit understanding |

### Traditional affinities

**Signs of the same element** have a natural affinity: they understand each other instinctively, speak the same emotional language, share the same life rhythms. An Aries and a Sagittarius can get along wonderfully because both need adventure and novelty. A Taurus and a Virgo share a taste for the concrete and for stability.

**Compatible elements** form two great dyads:
- **Fire + Air**: air feeds fire. Communication and ideas stimulate action and enthusiasm. It is an intellectual and energetic dynamic.
- **Earth + Water**: water fertilizes earth. Emotions give depth to realization. It is a sensual and nourishing dynamic.

**Elements in tension**:
- **Fire + Water**: steam rises from it. These couples can be passionate but exhausting. Fire burns the emotional, water extinguishes the momentum.
- **Earth + Air**: dust in the wind. Earth finds air superficial, air finds earth smothering. But when it works, it is very complementary.

These broad rules are useful as a starting reading grid, but they are far from enough to determine real compatibility. For that, you have to go into the detail of birth charts.

## Beyond sun signs: real compatibility

Here is the truth few people dare to say about mainstream horoscopes: **your sun sign alone does not determine even 5% of compatibility with someone**. What really matters are the positions of the other personal planets, and above all their interactions between your chart and your partner's.

### The 5 placements that make a difference in love

**Your Venus** represents what you love, how you love, what type of person attracts you. A Venus in Taurus seeks sensuality and stability. A Venus in Gemini seeks intellectual stimulation and lightness. A Venus in Scorpio seeks intense passion and exclusivity.

**Your Mars** represents how you desire, how you express your drive. Mars is particularly important for the sexual and physical dimension of a couple. A Mars in Aries is direct and conquering. A Mars in Pisces is romantic and merging. A Mars in Capricorn is structured and enduring.

**Your Moon** represents your deep emotional needs, what makes you feel "at home" with someone. It is often the Moon that determines whether a long-term relationship works, far more than the Sun. A Moon in Cancer needs softness and continuity. A Moon in Aquarius needs freedom and friendship.

**Your ascendant** determines how you present yourself to the world, and therefore the first impression you give, what attracts or repels from the start.

**Your seventh house** (the house of partnership) and the planet that rules it indicate the type of partner you attract in your committed relationships.

### The real question

The question therefore is not "are our sun signs compatible?" but rather: **"how does my Venus aspect his Mars? Does my Moon get along with his Moon? Does my Sun dialogue with his ascendant?"**

It is much more precise, much more useful, and explains why some "unlikely" couples (for example a Virgo and a Pisces, traditionally considered opposite) can have an absolutely magnificent synastry if their Moons and Venuses are well aligned.

## The 5 aspects that really matter in a couple

In synastry, astrologers look at **cross-aspects**: how a planet in your chart forms an angle with a planet in your partner's chart. Here are the five most important cross-aspects in love, in practical order of importance.

### 1. Sun A × Moon B (and vice versa)

This is **the king aspect of romantic synastry**. When your Sun forms a harmonious aspect (conjunction, trine, sextile) with your partner's Moon, there is an immediate feeling of "familiarity," as if you had known each other forever. You feel at home with this person. Most lasting couples have at least one Sun-Moon cross-aspect.

### 2. Venus A × Mars B (and vice versa)

This is the aspect of **physical and erotic attraction**. Venus represents beauty, Mars represents desire. When they meet between two people, the alchemy is palpable. A trine or conjunction Venus-Mars explains why you physically "click" with someone from the first meeting.

### 3. Moon A × Moon B

The **daily emotional compatibility**. Two Moons in good aspect (same sign, same element, or trine/sextile) make living together very easy. The little rituals, the moods, the life rhythms naturally align. Two Moons in square or opposition can create permanent friction around divergent emotional needs.

### 4. Saturn A × personal planet B

Saturn brings **duration and responsibility**. A well-aspected Saturn to a personal planet of the other indicates a couple that will last over time, that will know how to build together. But be careful: Saturn in tension (square, opposition) can also block the relationship, create weight, oppressive duty. The difference between "supportive" and "oppressive" hangs by one degree.

### 5. Pluto A × Venus or Mars B

Pluto brings **deep transformation and obsession**. Couples with a Pluto-Venus or Pluto-Mars aspect rarely live lukewarm stories. These are intense relationships, sometimes destructive, often metamorphosing. You rarely leave them the same way you entered.

## The unlikely couples that work

History and literature are full of couples that "should not work" according to traditional astrology, but work wonderfully. Here are a few revealing configurations.

### Virgo + Pisces (opposition)

On paper, one is pragmatic and analytical, the other dreamy and emotional. In practice, this couple often works as a complementary mirror: the Virgo brings the structure the Pisces lacks, the Pisces brings the soul the Virgo lacks. Zodiacal oppositions are often polarizing but magnetic when both partners accept their complementarity.

### Aries + Capricorn (square)

The Aries wants to charge, the Capricorn wants to plan. Permanent conflict? Or explosive collaboration? If the Capricorn accepts the Aries' spontaneity, and the Aries respects the Capricorn's structure, they can build something very solid. The zodiacal square is the most powerful engine of the zodiac: it forces movement, adaptation, growth.

### Cancer + Aquarius (quincunx)

Emotional versus detached. Home versus freedom. But these two signs can create a couple where Aquarius brings oxygen to the fusional tendency of Cancer, and Cancer brings warmth to the sometimes cool Aquarius. Many lasting couples have this dynamic.

### Scorpio + Gemini (quincunx)

Depth versus lightness. Exclusivity versus flirting. Yet Gemini can give breathing room to Scorpio who tends to dramatize everything, and Scorpio can teach Gemini emotional depth. When it works, it is a very interesting alliance of mind and soul.

## FAQ

### Can you be compatible with your opposite sign?

Yes, and it is often very interesting. Zodiacal oppositions (Aries-Libra, Taurus-Scorpio, Gemini-Sagittarius, Cancer-Capricorn, Leo-Aquarius, Virgo-Pisces) are polar: the two signs embody complementary energies that balance each other. These couples can create beautiful mirror dynamics where each brings to the other what they lack. The challenge is to accept this complementarity instead of wanting the other to be like us.

### Are sites that give a "compatibility score" reliable?

They are useful as a starting indication, but not as a verdict. A score of 85/100 can correspond to a relationship dying because no one maintains it. A score of 45/100 can correspond to an extraordinary story if both people do the necessary work. Automated scores cannot capture the conscious and deliberate dimension of a couple.

### How many signs does a truly solid couple have in aspect?

There is no absolute rule, but empirically lasting couples generally show at least 5-8 major cross-aspects between their charts, including at least one involving Sun-Moon, one involving Venus-Mars, and one involving Saturn to a personal planet. Less than that, the "thread woven" between the two people may be missing.

### Should I avoid someone with a tense synastry with me?

No, absolutely not. Tense synastries are often the ones that generate the most growth. Many great love stories in literature, cinema, and real life are based on complex synastries. The question is not "is it easy?" but "does it bring me what I need to grow?" Only a synastry with Saturn or Pluto tension on your Moon can really be long-term exhausting without inner work.

### Does compatibility change over time?

The natal synastry stays fixed, but **current transits** can temporarily activate dormant aspects and create particularly happy or particularly difficult phases in a couple. That is why a couple that worked well for years can go through a crisis when Saturn transits a sensitive point of the synastry. These phases pass.

### Can I know my compatibility without knowing his time of birth?

Partially. Without his time, you will not have his ascendant or the exact positions of the houses. You will still be able to calculate aspects between personal planets (Sun, Moon for days without sign changes, Mercury, Venus, Mars) which already give a very good overview.
